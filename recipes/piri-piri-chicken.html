i n g r e d i e n t s



  • 2 Red Peppers
  • 2 Red Onions
  • 2 Red Serrano
    Chillies
  • 10 Birds Eye
    Chillies
  • 1 Lemon
    (Juice & Zest)
  • 1/2 a Cup of Oil
  • 4 Tbsp of Vinegar
  • 4 Cloves of Garlic
  • 1 Tsp of Paprika
  • 1 Tsp of Smoked
    Paprika
  • 2 Tsp of Cayenne
    Pepper
  • 1 1/2 Tsp of Salt
  • 1/4 Tsp of White Pepper
  • 1/4 Tsp of Dried
    Rosemary
  • 1 Medium Chicken
  • 1/2 Tsp of Black Pepper


m e t h o d



  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/ 350F/
    Gas 4.
  2. Place the peppers and red onion on a
    baking tray and drizzle half of the oil over
    them.
  3. Now, sprinkle on the paprika, smoked paprika,
    cayenne pepper, and salt then toss and place in
    the oven for 20 minutes.
  4. After the 20 minutes, add the garlic and
    serrano chillies and toss once more.
  5. Place in the oven again for a further 10
    minutes.
  6. Remove from the oven and transfer into a
    blender, making sure to use all the liquid from
    the tray.
  7. Add the dried chillies, white pepper, lemon zest,
    lemon juice, and the dried rosemary.
  8. Blend until smooth, then add the remaining oil
    and vinegar and continue blending.
  9. Place the chicken breast-side down on a
    sturdy chopping board, with the legs facing you.
  10. Prepare the chicken well by spatch-cocking it.
  11. Put the spatch-cocked chicken on a baking tray
    and pour over a third of the marinade.
  12. Place in the oven for 45 minutes.
  13. After 45 minutes, baste the chicken and spoon
    over some more sauce where needed.
  14. Put the chicken back in the oven for a further
    10-15 minutes, until it's tender.
  15. Remove from the oven and allow to rest for 10
    minutes.
  16. Serve with the remaining piri piri sauce.
  17. Enjoy!
