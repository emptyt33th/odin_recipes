i n g r e d i e n t s

  • 125g of Plain or All Purpose Flour
  • 100g of White Sugar
  • Yolks of 4 Fresh Large Eggs
  • Whites of 4 Fresh Large Eggs
  • 60g of Melted Butter
  • 18 Strawberries
  • 400ml of Double Cream
  • 40ml of Water
    • Here is the recipe that inspired this:

m e t h o d

  1. Preheat your oven to 180C/ 356F/ Gas 4.
  2. Coat the inside of a cake tin with butter and dust with flour, shaking off excess.
  3. Put the egg yolks in a bowl and add 2/3 of the 100g sugar and beat them both until white and thick.
  4. In a separate bowl, beat the egg whites, until you see the volume double.
  5. When small bubbles begin to arise, add the remaining sugar bit by bit.
  6. The meringue will be ready when it is firm and can form peaks.
  7. Put the flour through a sifter, and add to the beaten egg yolk.
  8. Fold the flour in with a spatula, and begin to add the meringue to this bit by bit.
  9. Continue folding with the spatula until combined.
  10. Now, pour the melted butter into the batter and fold several times.
  11. Pour the batter into a cake tin and drop the tin on a work bench to settle it.
  12. Bake in the oven for 25 minutes and once fully cooked, allow to cool.
  13. Whip the double cream until soft peaks form, and begin preparing the strawberries.
  14. Alternate the cream, strawberries, and cake until there are two levels to the cake.
  15. Set in the fridge for 2 hours.
  16. Enjoy!
